# Ghostblood Scholar

## Introduction

Not every Ghostblood operative is a fighter. The organization's ambitions on Roshar -- securing Oathgates, harvesting Stormlight, studying the Unmade, imprisoning Heralds -- require a deep understanding of Rosharan Investiture and cosmere mechanics that most enforcers and spies simply lack. Ghostblood scholars fill this role: cosmere-aware researchers who study the interactions between Scadrial's metallic arts and Roshar's surges, catalog gemstone properties, analyze spren behavior, and decode ancient texts.

Most scholars are recruited from Scadrial's academic institutions, though some are Rosharan converts who proved their intellect and loyalty. They carry notebooks filled with coded observations, specimen jars of captured spren essence, and small collections of allomantic metals for experimental purposes. In the field, they are rarely alone -- a scholar's knowledge is too valuable to risk without an enforcer or spy escort.

When cornered, a scholar relies on ingenuity over martial skill. They carry concealed knives and minor Scadrian devices, and their understanding of Investiture gives them an unsettling ability to identify and exploit their enemies' magical weaknesses.

*"The locals call it Stormlight. They infuse it into gemstones and think that's the extent of its utility. They have no idea what we could do with a reliable supply."*

## Features

**Minion.** The scholar's attacks can't critically hit, and they are immediately defeated when they suffer an injury.

**Cosmere-Aware.** The scholar has studied the fundamental forces of the cosmere. They gain an advantage on Lore and Deduction tests related to Investiture, Surgebinding, the metallic arts, or other cosmere phenomena. They can identify the type and source of Investiture being used by a character within 30 feet as a free action.

**Analytical Eye.** After the scholar observes a character use an Invested ability (such as a surge, Allomantic power, or fabrial), the scholar can call out a weakness. The next attack made by the scholar's ally against that character gains an advantage.

## Actions

**▶ Strike: Concealed Knife.** Attack +4, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Graze:* 2 (1d4) keen damage. *Hit:* 6 (1d4 + 4) keen damage.

**▶ Counter Argument.** During a conversation, the scholar makes a Persuasion test against a character's Spiritual defense. On a success, the target loses 3 focus.

**▶▶ Identify Vulnerability (Costs 1 Focus).** The scholar studies one enemy they can sense within 30 feet. Until the end of the scholar's next turn, that enemy's defenses are reduced by 2 against the next attack made by the scholar or one of the scholar's allies.

## Tactics

A **Ghostblood scholar** avoids direct combat whenever possible, staying behind enforcer and spy escorts. They use Analytical Eye and Identify Vulnerability to support their allies, calling out weaknesses in enemies' techniques. If drawn into conversation or negotiation, they use Counter Argument to drain opponents' resolve. They fight with a Concealed Knife only as a last resort and flee when their escorts fall.
