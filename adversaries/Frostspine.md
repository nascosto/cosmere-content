# Frostspine

## Introduction

Frostspines are a larger, hardier relative of the common whitespine, found only in the remote heights of the Mountains of Ur. They are all but unknown to the outside world -- few who wander deep enough into those peaks return to speak of them.

Like whitespines, Frostspines move on two legs with two sets of clawed arms, and a ridge of bony spikes runs the length of their carapace. Their most striking feature is their skin: an iridescent, shifting blue-green reminiscent of a glacial lake under open sky. Against snow and bare rock, this coloring breaks up their silhouette, making them deceptively difficult to spot for their size.

Frostspines are ambush hunters, patient and territorial. They hunt in small packs, staking out narrow mountain passes and high ridgelines where prey has nowhere to run, then charging with devastating force. Their thick carapace sheds blows that would fell a whitespine, and their roar -- a deep, reverberating bellow that carries for miles through mountain valleys -- has been mistaken for rockslides by the few travelers who have heard it and lived.

What little is known about them comes from Unkalaki hunters who speak of them with a mixture of respect and dread. No Frostspine has ever been captured alive, and no tusk has ever made its way to a lighteyed trophy collection. To most of Roshar, they are nothing more than a Horneater legend.

## Features

**Enhanced Senses.** The Frostspine gains an advantage on non-attack tests that rely on smell.

**Spined Carapace.** After an enemy within 5 feet of the Frostspine grazes them with a weapon attack, that enemy takes 3 (1d6) keen damage.

**Alpine Predator.** The Frostspine ignores difficult terrain caused by snow, ice, or rocky slopes.

## Actions

**Strike: Claws.** Attack +8, reach 5 ft., one target. *Graze:* 3 (1d6) keen damage. *Hit:* 11 (1d6 + 8) keen damage, and the Frostspine can spend 1 focus to make the target Afflicted [1d4 vital] until the target regains at least 1 health.

**Avalanche Charge.** Attack +8, reach 5 ft., one target. *Graze:* 5 (1d10) impact damage. *Hit:* 13 (1d10 + 8) impact damage. If the Frostspine hits with this attack immediately after moving at least 20 feet, the target and each enemy adjacent to the target take an extra 5 (1d10) impact damage and must succeed on a DC 14 Athletics test or be knocked Prone.

**Terrifying Roar (Costs 1 Focus).** The Frostspine unleashes a reverberating bellow that echoes off the mountain stone. Each non-Frostspine character within 15 feet must make a DC 15 Discipline test. On a failure, the target loses 2 focus. On a success, the target loses 1 focus.

## Tactics

As a dangerous predator, a **Frostspine** stalks its prey through mountain passes, using its Alpine Predator feature to close distance across treacherous terrain. Frostspines hunt in small packs, flanking prey from multiple ridgelines before charging in with Avalanche Charge to scatter and knock down their targets. They follow up with Claws on prone or isolated enemies, leaving them bleeding out. If the pack meets stiff resistance, one Frostspine uses Terrifying Roar to drain the group's focus while the others press the attack.
