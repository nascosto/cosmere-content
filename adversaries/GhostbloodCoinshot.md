# Ghostblood Coinshot

## Introduction

Among the Ghostbloods' most dangerous operatives on Roshar are those who carry the power of Scadrial in their blood. Coinshots -- Allomancers who burn steel to Push on nearby metals -- are prized agents, capable of turning a handful of clips into a storm of lethal projectiles. On Scadrial, a Coinshot is a known quantity; on Roshar, where few understand Allomancy, they are something else entirely: inexplicable, terrifying, and nearly impossible to counter without cosmere-awareness.

Ghostblood Coinshots typically operate in small cells, providing ranged support for heists, assassinations, and the retrieval of Investiture-rich artifacts. They carry pouches of metal coins and often wear minimal metal themselves to avoid being Pushed or Pulled by rival Allomancers. Their fighting style is precise and clinical -- they prefer to keep distance, peppering targets with coins before launching themselves skyward off anchored metal to reposition.

To Rosharans unfamiliar with the cosmere, a Coinshot's abilities are baffling. The coins seem to fly of their own volition, and the Coinshot's leaping movements defy normal explanation. Some mistake them for Surgebinders; others assume fabrials are involved. The Ghostbloods encourage this confusion.

*"Three diamond tattoos on the wrist. Foreign coins in the wounds. Whatever killed these men, it wasn't a Shardblade."*

## Features

**Steelsight.** The Ghostblood Coinshot can sense all sources of metal within 30 feet, appearing as faint blue lines in their vision. They gain an advantage on Perception tests to detect hidden creatures or objects that contain metal, and they can sense the presence of metallic equipment even through walls or cover.

**Allomantic Reserves.** The Ghostblood Coinshot's abilities are fueled by burning steel, represented by their Investiture pool. When the Coinshot's Investiture reaches 0, they can no longer use actions or features that cost Investiture.

**Steel Launch.** When the Ghostblood Coinshot uses the Move action and there is a metal object at least as heavy as them anchored to the ground or a structure within 10 feet, they can Push off it to increase their movement by 15 feet for that action. This movement can include vertical distance.

## Actions

**▶ Strike: Coin Shot.** Attack +8, range 60/120 ft., one target. *Graze:* 3 (1d6) keen damage. *Hit:* 11 (1d6 + 8) keen damage.

**▶ Coin Barrage (Costs 1 Investiture).** The Coinshot Pushes a spray of coins at high velocity. Each enemy in a 15-foot cone must succeed on a DC 15 Agility test or take 10 (2d6 + 3) keen damage. On a success, a target takes 3 (1d6) keen damage instead.

**▶ Steel Push (Costs 1 Investiture).** The Coinshot targets one creature they can sense within 30 feet that is wearing or carrying metal. The target must succeed on a DC 14 Athletics test or be Pushed 15 feet directly away from the Coinshot and knocked Prone. If the target is pushed into a wall or obstacle, they take 5 (1d10) impact damage.

**⮐ Coin Deflection (Costs 1 Focus).** Before the Coinshot is hit by a ranged weapon attack, they Push on the incoming projectile if it contains metal, increasing their Physical defense by 3 against that attack, potentially causing it to miss.

## Tactics

A **Ghostblood Coinshot** fights at range and uses mobility to stay out of melee. They open with Coin Barrage against grouped enemies to deal early damage, then follow up with individual Coin Shots against priority targets. They use Steel Push to keep melee attackers at bay and Steel Launch to reposition to elevated ground or across gaps. If cornered, a Coinshot uses Steel Push to create space and retreats. They fight methodically and withdraw when outmatched or when their Allomantic reserves run low -- Ghostblood operatives know their value to the organization and avoid dying over a single mission.
