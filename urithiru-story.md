## Beginning

**Brightlord Toradin:**
You three — the new Radiants, yes? I'm Brightlord Toradin, Highprince Hathan's exploration command. I'll keep this brief.

There's an unsurveyed section deep in the eastern substrata. We've sent two teams in. Neither came back — fourteen people, gone. Whatever's down there, regular folk can't handle it.

This is Lenari Dryl. She's volunteered to join you. Says she's looking for adventure. I guess this qualifies.

Given what we've lost already, the payout reflects the risk. A requisition to Highprince Hathan's armory — weapons, armor, and other odds and ends, very high quality including some newly discovered items found right here in Urithiru. Are you in? Of course you are!

## Briefing

**Brightlord Toradin:**
Here's a map of the previous section we've already surveyed. The area you're heading into is Section 15 — it's up against the mountain itself. There's a large balcony out there, so you won't be entirely boxed in. No guarantee the layout matches what we've already mapped, but it's the best reference we've got.

_He hands over a sealed document._

This is your Writ of Exploration and Authorization, signed by Highprince Hatham himself. It grants you full authority to enter, survey, and explore Section 15 — all chambers, passages, and balcony structures, right up to where the tower meets the mountain rock. You're authorized to clear obstructions, mark passages, and secure chambers. Anything you can't get through, just flag it and we'll send in experts later. We just need to know that it's safe. Any fabrials, artifacts, or items of interest — catalogue them and report to the surveyors' office. Nothing leaves without approval from the Highprince's steward.

One more thing. No one is allowed down there but you and your company. If you find anyone — anyone at all — you detain them and bring them in. No exceptions.

## Tree Room

You step through the doorway into a circular room — three stories tall, smooth stone walls, a spiral staircase climbing the outer edge to an open doorway above.

A massive tree fills the center. Its canopy spreads across the upper stories, veins of blue-green light pulsing through the bark and along the branches, casting shifting light across the walls. Black fruit hangs heavy in clusters from the limbs.

A buzzing sound fills the air.

As you look closer, you spot beetles crawling along the bark and through the leaves — dark green and dark blue, their shells catching the glow. The buzzing is coming from them. They don't react to your presence.

As you listen, you realize the buzzing is two distinct sounds — one higher, one lower, layered over each other.

## Upstairs Room

The spiral staircase leads to a small room off the upper doorway. A desk sits against the wall — and it's been used recently. An inkwell is uncapped, notes are spread out, and a notebook lies open. The pages are filled with dense, careful handwriting in a language none of you recognize. Someone has been working here.

## Mysterious Door

As you enter the room, you notice a metal door set into the far wall. No handle, no place for a key. Its surface is covered in a geometric pattern you aren't familiar with.

As you get close, almost invisible lines become visible running across the metal — thin and deliberate. Some kind of fabrial work. None of you have any idea what they mean.

## Plaza

You step out onto a snow-covered stone balcony that stretches roughly two hundred feet to the cliff's edge. The sky is heavy with clouds — you can't see much beyond the drop and the balcony is covered in snow.

Near the center, a small stone podium rises from the ground — maybe two feet across. Gemstones are set into its surface, light rising from them about a foot into the air, shifting between colors.

When you touch one of the gemstones, a clear tone rings out — like an instrument — and a line of light the same color shoots out from the podium, stretching maybe fifteen feet across the balcony before fading. Different stones, different tones.

It's just a plaza. The podium is some kind of musical game — something the residents of Urithiru must have used for fun.

As you turn to head back inside, a feeling of dread hits you. Three shapes between you and the entrance. Whitespines — no. These are bigger. Their skin is an iridescent, shifting blue-green, like a glacial lake under open sky, with ridges of big white spikes running down their backs. They're near the tower wall, between you and the way back in. They look hungry.

## Floating Room

You step into a stone room — fifty feet wide and eighty feet long, with a fifty-foot ceiling. A large set of double doors sits on the far wall. Before you can take it in, the door behind you shuts. The corners of the room light up, and your feet leave the ground. All of you drift toward the center of the room, weightless. Every surface is just far enough away that reaching one won't be easy.
